Why Mill Test Reports Matter

A Mill Test Report is the manufacturer's certified record of the chemical composition, mechanical properties, and heat or batch number of the material supplied. It is the document that proves a pipe, fitting, valve, or flange actually meets the grade and standard it is sold as — verified by laboratory testing, not just labelling. For engineering, procurement, and QA teams, the MTR is what allows material to be traced back to its original heat and mill, which is essential for accountability in pressure piping, process plants, and any application where failure is not an option.

Every product line supplied by Murtaza Corporation is backed by an MTR issued in accordance with EN 10204 (Type 3.1), verifying compliance with international standards such as ASTM, ASME, and DIN. Maintaining these records means our customers can move into inspection, welding, or installation with full confidence in material provenance, and it means our supply meets the documentation standards expected by international buyers, EPC contractors, and regulatory bodies.

Importance to the Company & International Standards

1

Quality Assurance & Accountability

MTRs give Murtaza Corporation a documented, auditable chain of custody for every material we sell — from mill to end customer. This protects customers from substandard or mismatched material and protects the company by keeping every batch fully traceable.

Standard: EN 10204 – Type 3.1 Inspection Certificate
2

International Compliance

Global buyers, EPC contractors, and regulatory bodies require documented proof that material meets the ASTM, ASME, or DIN grade specified in a purchase order. Supplying certified MTRs allows our products to move freely into regulated industries such as oil & gas, power generation, desalination, and petrochemicals.

Standards: ASTM · ASME · DIN
