Stainless & Carbon Steel Flange Systems

A flange serves as a method for connecting pipes, valves, pumps, and additional equipment to create a piping system. It is primarily employed in situations where a connection or disassembly point is required. These joints serve the purpose of linking a pipe to fittings, valves, mechanical equipment, or other essential components within a piping system. Moreover, they provide easy access for cleaning, inspection, or modification. Typically, flanges are either welded or screwed into place.

Stainless steel flanges, produced to ASTM A182 grades F304/304L/316/316L/321/310/347 and similar, offer excellent corrosion resistance for harsh, corrosive or hygienic environments, while carbon steel flanges — to ASTM A105, A694 F42 through F70, and A350 LF2/LF3 — remain a cost-effective, weld-friendly choice where corrosion resistance is not the primary concern. Both are stocked across the full range of sizes, wall thicknesses, facings and pressure classes to suit pipeline design requirements across petrochemical, oil & gas, power generation and general industrial applications.

Flange Types

1

Weld Neck & Long Weld Neck Flanges

Identified by a long tapered hub that is butt-welded to the pipe, weld neck flanges transfer stress from the flange to the pipe, reducing high stress concentrations at the base of the hub. Long weld neck flanges extend this hub further, doubling as a schedule pipe section and reducing the number of welded joints in a line. Both are the preferred choice for high-pressure, high-temperature and cyclic-load piping.

Spec: ASTM A182 F304/F304L/F316/F316L/F321/F310/F347 (SS) · ASTM A105, A694 F42-F70, A350 LF2/LF3 (CS) · ANSI B16.5 / B16.47
2

Slip-On Flanges

Slip-on flanges slide over the pipe end and are fillet-welded both inside and outside for added strength. Easier to align than weld neck flanges and lower in initial cost, they are widely used in low-pressure applications where ease of installation outweighs the need for maximum joint strength.

Spec: ASTM A182 F304/F304L/F316/F316L (SS) · ASTM A105 (CS) · ANSI B16.5 / MSS-SP-44
3

Socket Weld Flanges

Designed with a recessed bore into which the pipe is inserted and single fillet-welded around the outside, socket weld flanges give a smoother bore than slip-on types, making them well suited to smaller-bore, high-pressure piping carrying non-corrosive fluids and gases.

Spec: ASTM A182 F304/F304L/F316/F316L (SS) · ASTM A105 (CS) · ANSI B16.5
4

Blind Flanges

Blind flanges have no bore and are used to seal off the end of a piping system, a valve, or a pressure vessel opening. They also provide a convenient point of access for future line extension.

Spec: ASTM A182 F304/F304L/F316/F316L (SS) · ASTM A105 (CS) · ANSI B16.5 / B16.47
5

Orifice Flanges

Manufactured in weld neck or slip-on styles with radial tapped and jack-screw holes, orifice flanges are used in pairs to hold an orifice plate in place for flow measurement, and permit the plate to be removed for inspection without breaking the pipeline alignment.

Spec: ASTM A182 F304/F304L/F316/F316L (SS) · ASTM A105 (CS) · ANSI B16.36
6

Ring Type Joint (RTJ) Flanges

RTJ flanges carry a machined groove on the face into which a metal ring gasket seats, forming a metal-to-metal seal under bolt load. This facing is specified for high-pressure and high-temperature service where a superior, leak-tight seal is required, commonly in upstream oil & gas piping.

Spec: ASTM A182 F304/F304L/F316/F316L (SS) · ASTM A105 (CS) · ANSI B16.5
7

Raised Face & Flat Face Flanges

Raised face flanges carry a small raised area around the bore that concentrates bolt load onto a smaller gasket area, and are the most common facing for steel piping systems. Flat face flanges, machined flush across the whole face, are typically specified when mating with cast iron flanges or fittings that cannot tolerate a raised face without risk of cracking.

Spec: ASTM A182 F304/F304L/F316/F316L (SS) · ASTM A105 (CS) · ANSI B16.5
